\unchapter{Abstract}

This Bachelor's thesis examines the integration maturity of information systems 
within Project Management Office (PMO) environments in medium and large Latvian 
enterprises. As organizations increasingly rely on multiple digital tools for 
project planning, reporting, and governance, the degree to which these systems 
are integrated has a direct impact on data quality, reporting efficiency, and 
governance standardization. Despite its practical relevance, this topic has 
received limited empirical attention in the Latvian context.

The Bachelor Paper consists of four chapters. Chapter 1 reviews the theoretical 
background, defining key concepts such as PMO, information system integration, 
maturity models, business intelligence, and data quality, and surveys existing 
research on PMO digitalization and integration maturity frameworks. Chapter 2 
presents the research methodology, including the design of a four-level 
Integration Maturity Model and the development of a structured survey instrument. 
Chapter 3 contains the empirical study, presenting the results of a survey 
conducted among representatives of Latvian enterprises with formal PMO structures, 
supplemented by expert interviews and statistical analysis. Chapter 4 proposes 
practical recommendations for improving information system integration maturity 
in PMO environments based on the empirical findings.

The methodological basis of the study includes systematic literature review, 
survey-based quantitative research, structured expert interviews, descriptive 
statistical analysis, and correlation and regression analysis.

The study is expected to reveal that most PMO environments in Latvia operate at 
intermediate integration maturity levels, and that higher integration maturity 
is associated with greater reporting automation, better data quality, and stronger 
governance standardization. The findings are intended to provide evidence-based 
guidance for organizations seeking to improve their project management information 
systems.
