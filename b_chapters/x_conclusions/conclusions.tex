\unchapter{Conclusions}

This thesis set out to assess the integration maturity of information 
systems within Project Management Office environments in medium and 
large Latvian enterprises, and to examine how this maturity level 
relates to reporting automation, data quality, and governance 
standardization. The following section confirms that all research 
tasks have been completed, evaluates the four hypotheses, and 
concludes whether the aim of the thesis has been achieved.

\textbf{Confirmation of research tasks.}

Task 1 was completed in Chapter~1, which provided a systematic 
review of existing literature on PMO digitalization, information 
system integration architectures, and maturity models relevant 
to IS environments. This review established the theoretical 
foundation for the entire study.

Task 2 was fulfilled in Chapter~1, where a four-level Integration 
Maturity Model was developed and operationalized with clearly 
defined, measurable criteria for each level, covering integration 
mechanisms, automation level, reporting structure, and BI 
centralization.

Task 3 was completed in Chapter~2, which presented the design 
and validation of a structured survey instrument containing 
Likert-scale and categorical questions aligned with the 
Integration Maturity Model dimensions.

Task 4 was accomplished through the distribution of the survey 
to representatives of medium and large Latvian enterprises with 
50 or more employees and formal PMO structures. A total of 40 
valid responses were collected, meeting the target of 30--50 
responses set out in the methodology.

Task 5 was carried out in Chapter~3, where one structured expert 
interview was conducted with an experienced project management 
professional to supplement the survey findings with qualitative 
insights and architectural depth.

Task 6 was completed in Chapter~3, where descriptive statistical 
analysis was performed to determine the distribution of integration 
maturity levels across the sampled PMO environments.

Task 7 was fulfilled in Chapter~3, where Spearman correlation 
and simple regression analysis were applied to examine the 
relationships between integration maturity and reporting 
automation, data accuracy, and governance standardization.

Task 8 was completed in Chapter~4, where the findings were 
interpreted, conclusions were drawn regarding all four hypotheses, 
and five practical recommendations for PMO information system 
integration improvement in Latvian enterprises were formulated.

\textbf{Validation of hypotheses.}

All four research hypotheses were supported by the empirical 
evidence collected in this study.

Hypothesis 1 was confirmed. The survey results showed that 70\% 
of the sampled PMO environments operate at intermediate integration 
maturity levels — Level 2 (Developing) or Level 3 (Defined) — 
rather than at the most advanced level. Only 15\% of organizations 
had reached Level 4, confirming that most Latvian PMOs are still 
in a transitional phase of IS integration development.

Hypothesis 2 was confirmed. A statistically significant positive 
correlation was found between integration maturity and reporting 
automation (r=0.405, p=0.010). This demonstrates that organizations 
with higher IS integration maturity consistently report higher 
levels of automated reporting, reducing the reliance on manual 
data consolidation.

Hypothesis 3 was confirmed. A statistically significant positive 
correlation was found between integration maturity and data quality 
(r=0.321, p=0.043). While this was the weakest of the three 
correlations, it confirms that better integrated systems are 
associated with higher perceived data accuracy and timeliness 
in PMO reporting.

Hypothesis 4 was confirmed. A statistically significant positive 
correlation was found between integration maturity and governance 
standardization (r=0.403, p=0.010). Organizations with higher 
integration maturity demonstrated notably stronger governance 
standardization practices, supported by the expert interview 
finding that centralized BI dashboards played a transformative 
role in standardizing governance processes.

\textbf{Achievement of the aim.}

The aim of this thesis has been fully achieved. The study 
successfully assessed the IS integration maturity of PMO 
environments in medium and large Latvian enterprises and 
demonstrated clear, statistically significant relationships 
between integration maturity and all three dependent variables. 
The findings confirm that most Latvian PMO environments operate 
at intermediate maturity levels and that higher integration 
maturity is consistently linked to better reporting automation, 
data quality, and governance standardization. Therefore, it 
can be concluded that the aim of the thesis has been reached.

\textbf{Practical and theoretical value.}

This study contributes to both practice and theory. On the 
practical side, five evidence-based recommendations were 
developed to help Latvian enterprises improve their PMO IS 
integration maturity. These include prioritizing API-based 
integration, investing in centralized BI dashboards, adopting 
low-code platforms suitable for medium-sized organizations, 
addressing interoperability between local Latvian accounting 
systems and global PM tools, and investing in data literacy 
training for project management staff.

On the theoretical side, this study provides the first empirical 
validation of a PMO-specific IS Integration Maturity Model in 
the Latvian enterprise context. It extends the existing body of 
research on digital maturity models by demonstrating that the 
maturity concept can be meaningfully applied to IS integration 
in PMO environments, and contributes new empirical evidence to 
the growing literature on digital transformation in Baltic and 
Eastern European enterprises.

All eight tasks set out in the Introduction were accomplished. 
Tasks 1 and 2 correspond to Chapter~1, Task 3 corresponds to 
Chapter~2, Tasks 4--7 correspond to Chapter~3, and Task 8 
corresponds to Chapter~4. All four research hypotheses were 
supported by the empirical data, and the aim of the thesis 
has been fully achieved.
