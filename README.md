# BSc Thesis – Assessment of Information System Integration 
# Maturity in Project Management Offices: Evidence from Latvian Enterprises

**Author:** Amina Showkath  
**Institution:** Riga Nordic University (RNU)  
**Supervisor:** PhD. Assoc. Prof. Andrejs Bondarenko  
**Year:** 2026 | **Programme:** BSc Information Systems (4220)

## Overview
This thesis empirically assessed the information system integration 
maturity of Project Management Offices (PMOs) in medium and large 
Latvian enterprises, examining its relationship with reporting 
automation, data quality, and governance standardization.

A self-designed four-level Integration Maturity Model (IMM) was 
developed and validated through a structured survey (n=40) and 
one expert interview, with Spearman rank correlation analysis 
conducted in SPSS.

## Key Findings
- 70% of Latvian PMOs operate at intermediate integration maturity 
  (Levels 2–3)
- Higher integration maturity significantly correlates with:
  - Reporting automation (r=0.405, p=0.010)
  - Data quality (r=0.321, p=0.043)
  - Governance standardization (r=0.403, p=0.010)
- All four research hypotheses were supported

## Methods
Systematic literature review · Survey-based quantitative research ·  
Expert interview · Descriptive statistics · Spearman rank correlation · SPSS
